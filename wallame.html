WallaMe Redesign

2018.07

App Re-design

Role

  • UI Design

What is WallaMe?

WallaMe is an augmented reality and location-based app, which can leave AR message or watch somewhere in a real place.

Objectives

  • The targeted audience is people around the world.
  • The purpose of the wall and map is to let users explore more spots and posts.
  • The user goal: to explore more interesting posts from wall and map.
  • The business goal: undefined.

Problem Definition

For this app, I assume that people use it often for their social needs. Nevertheless, I still wanted to know why people use this app, what’s important to them, and what are their core needs.

Research

User Testing

I didn’t have access to any analytics of the app usage. So I conducted a user testing of the current app to discover how users interact with it and what pain points they have.

  • Most users didn't like the color matching and font design of this app. They thought that was an old-fashioned design
  • The layout is too crowded and distracted the users since there's no space between posts
  • Too many buttons and icons confused the users — it wasn’t clear what it’s about and how it works.
  • The wall map has no filter for user to find their interseted posts.
  • Most user thought the AR posts experience are useless and have no fun, so they rather use the timeline wall only.

Solution

I change the theme color to white, and keep the color matching as simple as it could be. The concise and flat design could make this interface as a more modern design.

For layout, I adjusted the space between posts, re-grouped all text information of one post, and remove redundant icons to make it more simple.

I add text instructions in the bottom navigation bar, changing the icons of function bar to text, and add filter to the wall map, which could make users explore posts more conveniently.

However, how could the AR works is still a problem for my re-design.

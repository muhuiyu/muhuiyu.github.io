About This Project

This is a challenge from Daily UI (Day 132), and my design goal is to design the home page and one category page of clothes shopping website. So at the beginning, we have to define the user needs.

As a customer, what information would I want to get from this website?
  • I want to find some categories or products easily
  • I want to know the popular or hot products
  • I want to know any special event or sales
  • Sometimes, I just want to go surfing on the website and find something new
  • I want to know all categories or any interesting categories (optional)

Reference Websites

After that, I choose some clothes shopping websites which I usually go to, comparing those interfaces and features. The common features of these website and their case studies are as following.

The Common Features
    Home Page
  • One page & flat design
  • Always has big and attractive banners on home page to show latest news and sales
  • Clean and Light Design (usually using simple colors for websites to bring out colorful product pictures)
  • Always put navigation bar and main categories bar on left or top sides (I assume that is a kind of habit of reading since people used to reading from left to right, so the important messages should be put on the left or left-top side)
    Category Page
  • Left column for user requests, and right column for product displaying
  • Have to design an attractive banner with simple animation (like slider) for the home page.

ZALORA (Website)

The home page of ZALORA has clear interface designed in simple color matching and an attractive banner picture.




Although ZALORA has such a well design, there still are some uncoordinated components in this website, like this HTML-default-style button above.




Also, the layout of text and pictures is not approperiate as the example below.




In conclusion, the features of ZALORA website are:

  • This product list shows available size choices, which is convenient to users.
  • Put coupon label on product pictures directly → but not significant.
  • The home page has too many information → not clear
  • There is plenty of room for design improvement.

GU (Website)

GU uses lots of colorful product pictures to decorate their website and show their product diversification. Also, the coordiation recommendations might also be an incentive for user to purchase their products.




however, there are too many text messages between pictures and make it a little bit messy.




To Conclude, the features of GU website are:

  • clean theme with colorful pictures
  • coordiation recommendations and tutorials for beginners
  • Contain too much text information in the website

NET (Website)

The design of NET is more concise with only two main colors, white and gray. Without redundant sales or text messages, it's pretty user-friendly.

A tiny mistake is that the search bar has an unappropriate layout.




In conclusion, the features of NET website design are:

  • Completely minimalist design and well-designed
  • The design is composed by plenty of squares, which is tidy but boring
  • Provide color list with each product for users

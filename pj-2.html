UX DESIGN

Group Buying Feature Process

Overview
Blay App is a Flight Assistant and friend-making app for users to meet travelers all over the world. It is the world's first app to let you see who is flying to the same destination on the same day as you right after putting in your flight information.

Recently, Blay is making proactive development to create novel social situations. As a consequence, they focused on the social potential of “group buying”, and built up this function as a new feature to make travelers get more social interactions.

The Solution
In this project, I took references from 拼多多 (Group-Buying More) App, Shopee App, Gomaji App, and Deliveroo App. After collecting and assessing the user activities of Blay App, I completed a whole user flow and interactions of Group Buying feature in Blay App.

THE CHALLENGE

My Role

As the UX designer, I started from product specification definition, user flow, wireframe, and low-fi prototyping. Also, I did some usability testing by interviewing to our team members and real users.

Success Matrics

  • Have group buying events and coupons in app
  • Should have different authorities for admins

TARGET AUDIENCE

Our Users

The users of Blay App are travelers. Users can simply put in their flight numbers and get subscription for essential real-time information. Furthermore, users can see other travelers flying to the same destination on the same day as them and have interactions. Most users of Blay App would love to make new friends, or just love with the amazing app interface and wonderful user experience.

BUSINESS STRATEGY

Group Buying Marketing Strategy

First, Blay wants to put more emphasis on social connection and fabric. Secondly, Blay hopes this feature would be a new marketing strategy and create more profits in this app with other partnership companies and business.

USER RESERACH

IMPLEMENT

Usability Test

We took usability tests twice. One is for wireframe prototyping, and the other is for our high-fidelity prototyping.

First Testing

For the first time, we only ran the usability test on our team members (including UI designer, business developer, product manager, and back-end engineer) and potential users. Our feedbacks is as follows:

  • Great and smooth user flow to choosing / joining a new group buying event.
  • The notification bar is annoying while using this app.
  • Need more motivating things or events to make users click/view the group buying pages.

Second Testing

We will have the second usability testing after the prototype being finished.
